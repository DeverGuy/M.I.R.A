5. How M.I.R.A. Works (Architecture & Process Flow)
• Step 1: Global Data Ingestion (The Senses) — We continuously ingest massive amounts of raw, unstructured data. This includes high-resolution optical and radar imagery from Copernicus/ESA satellites, real-time localized rainfall data from global weather APIs, and historical soil profile databases. Because it's 100% remote, there is zero hardware installation required on the ground.
• Step 2: AI Processing Engine (The Brain) — This raw data is fed into our proprietary machine learning pipelines. Here, we run specialized algorithms to classify specific crop types, calculate complex vegetation indices like NDVI (plant health) and NDWI (water content), and synthesize this data over a rolling 12-week timeline to detect microscopic signs of moisture stress before it affects crop yields.
• Step 3: Actionable Insights (The Output) — The synthesized data is pushed to our sleek, responsive dashboard. Farmers are presented with easy-to-understand visual heatmaps, interactive field-level time-series charts, and automated, pinpoint irrigation advisories (e.g., "Optimal", "Irrigate Soon", "Critical Deficit") to make immediate, data-backed decisions.

6. Wireframes & Solution Interface (Dashboard Walkthrough)
• Main Navigation (Sidebar): Allows users to seamlessly toggle between the Crop Type Map, Moisture Stress layers, and AI Irrigation Advisory views.
• Interactive Global Map: The core visualization engine. Displays thousands of data points geographically. Users can zoom from a planetary view down to individual farm fields or draw custom regions.
• Field Analytics Panel: Clicking any field or drawing a custom region instantly opens a sleek data panel. This displays the 12-week NDVI/NDWI time-series charts, area statistics, and the precise AI advisory status.
• Crop Suggestion Engine: A dedicated UI component that processes the selected coordinate's climate and soil data to recommend the most profitable crop for that exact location.
